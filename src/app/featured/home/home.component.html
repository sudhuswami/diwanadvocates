Divorce

If you are preparing to go through a divorce, we can help you make informed decisions, and we will guide you through every step of the process. From advising you prior to separation and while anticipating divorce, to negotiating your property settlement and fighting for alimony and child custody, we will handle all aspects of your divorce strategically, aggressively, and with your long-term best interests in mind.

Custody

Whether you are divorcing, separating from your partner or dealing with a custody issue post judgment, the more effort you put into protecting your relationship with your children now, the less likely you are to face problems down the road. With extensive experience structuring mutually-agreeable parenting time plans and fighting for our clients’ custody and visitation rights in court, our attorneys know what it takes to make sure our clients can watch their children grow and be involved in their children’s lives on a frequent basis.

Child Support

In any divorce or separation, the calculation of child support is subject to Michigan’s Child Support Formula. While this formula is designed to make it easier for parents to calculate their support obligations, these calculations are often complex. We help our clients determine appropriate payment obligations and represent single mothers and fathers in child support modification and enforcement proceedings. These proceedings are often highly contentious, and obtaining a favorable outcome frequently requires aggressive litigation.

Parental Alienation

Parental alienation cases are among the most emotionally-charged and most contentious cases we handle. If you and your child are victims of parental alienation, or if you have been falsely accused of abuse, we can take swift legal action to protect you. Whether your child is exhibiting symptoms of parental alienation or you have not seen your child in months or years, we can use our decades of experience handling parental alienation cases in the Michigan courts to help you.

Appeals

In addition to representing our clients in trial, we also provide representation in civil appeals. While not all decisions are subject to appeal, if you have grounds to challenge the outcome of your divorce, child custody dispute or other family law case, our attorneys can appeal your trial verdict to the Michigan Court of Appeals.
